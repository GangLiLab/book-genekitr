9 ORA Additional

In this part, we will go through other practical ORA methods, including human disease enrichment analysis and WikiPathway analysis.

9.1 Available arguments

  • id: gene id of Entrez, Ensembl, symbol or Uniprot
  • type: choose one from do (Disease Ontology), ncg (Network of Cancer Gene) and dgn (DisGeNET)
  • group_list: gene set comparison groups, default is NULL.
  • pAdjustMethod: choose one from ‚Äúholm‚Äù, ‚Äúhochberg‚Äù, ‚Äúhommel‚Äù, ‚Äúbonferroni‚Äù, ‚ÄúBH‚Äù, ‚ÄúBY‚Äù, ‚Äúfdr‚Äù, ‚Äúnone‚Äù.
  • pvalueCutoff: pvalue cutoff, default is 0.05.
  • qvalueCutoff: adjusted pvalue cutoff, default is 0.05.
  • minGSSize: minimal size of each geneSet for analyzing, default is 10.
  • maxGSSize: maximal size of each geneSet for analyzing, default is 500. Program will choose gene set in range [minGSSize,maxGSSize] to analyze
  • universe: Background genes of Entrez, Ensembl, symbol or Uniprot. Default is NULL.

9.2 Human Disease Ontology (DO)

The Disease Ontology has been developed as a standardized ontology for human disease with the purpose of providing the biomedical community with consistent, reusable and sustainable descriptions of human disease terms.

9.4 Human DisGeNET (DGN)

DisGeNET is a discovery platform containing one of the largest publicly available collections of genes and variants associated to human diseases.

DisGeNET integrates data from expert curated repositories, GWAS catalogues, animal models and the scientific literature. DisGeNET data are homogeneously annotated with controlled vocabularies and community-driven ontologies. Additionally, several original metrics are provided to assist the prioritization of genotype‚Äìphenotype relationships.
